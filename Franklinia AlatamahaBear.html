Franklinia alatamaha

Franklinia alatamaha, commonly known as the Franklin tree, is an endangered tree native to the eastern United States, primarily along the Altamaha River in Georgia. The rare tree is known for its natural beauty, with its white camellia-like flowers and attractive, glossy green leaves that turn orange and red in autumn. Discovered by John Bartram and William Bartram in the 18th century, the Franklin Tree has an interesting history as it is not found in the wild and is known only through cultivation. Breeding and conservation efforts have been carried out to preserve the genetics of this species and prevent its extinction. Franklinia alatamaha is a testament to the importance of conservation and the role humans play in preserving animals and plants.
