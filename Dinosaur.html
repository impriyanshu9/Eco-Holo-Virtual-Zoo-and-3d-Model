Dinosaur

Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles that dominated the Earth for over 160 million years during the Mesozoic Era, from the Late Triassic period (about 230 million years ago) to the end of the Cretaceous period (about 65 million years ago). They evolved into a vast array of species with varying sizes, shapes, and ecological niches, ranging from small, agile predators to gigantic, long-necked herbivores. These ancient creatures are known for their unique features such as bipedal locomotion, impressive horns, frills, armor, and feathers. Dinosaurs' extinction, likely caused by a catastrophic event such as an asteroid impact or volcanic activity, paved the way for the rise of mammals and eventually led to the evolution of modern birds, which are considered direct descendants of some dinosaur groups. The study of dinosaurs continues to captivate scientists and enthusiasts alike, shedding light on Earth's prehistoric past and providing valuable insights into the evolution of life on our planet.
